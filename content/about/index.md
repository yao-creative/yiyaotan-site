  I'm the one on the left in the photo above :D!

  My name is Yao. Growing up, I've lived in 5 countries across a total of three continents.

  I've recently finished an MSc in Mathematics and Foundations of Computer Science at the University of Oxford, working in the Complexity Economics group led by Doyne Farmer (inventor of the first wearable computer, founder of the Prediction Company, and econophysicist) and supervised by Renaud Lambiotte (network community detection researcher - Louvain method). Previously, I graduated with a BSc in Mathematics and Computer Science from École Polytechnique (l'X) in Paris. My cohort was probably the most amazing group of people I've met. I also previously did research at Huawei, creating their sparse distributed attention library for NLP and image segmentation and classification in the MindSpore framework.
  
  As a regularly curious person, I'm also passionate about democratizing access to opportunity, continual learning, communication theory, languages, and inter/intra-personal psychology. I've dabbled in research on network analysis for supply chain reconstruction, transformer image segmentation and NLP, combinatorics, high-performance computing for distributed machine learning, and clustering analysis of cancer-related proteins (CDK2).

  Besides my work in mathematics and computer science, I enjoy playing in the orchestra and playing volleyball. At Oxford, I played the flute for the Hertford and Turl Street Orchestras. For volleyball, I've played for the Oxford University Blues team and Penang State back in Malaysia, with a silver in the national U21 competition. I've also coached middle school and high school volleyball and enjoy teaching. I enjoy great conversations with strangers, travel, photography, and literature from various languages.

  I'm pescatarian, which I believe is important to me, as it helps me make conscious decisions about my health and what I consume on a daily basis. In my free time, I enjoy listening to music, often classical, but at other times more diverse genres such as Italian rap or Japanese city pop. I also listen to a lot of podcasts and short book summaries, mostly on philosophy (Camus, Walter Benjamin, Sean Kelly), history, and some sci-fi fiction (Asimov), magical realism (Murakami), and poetry (Khalil Gibran). You can also find me crashing at my friends' places in different subcontinents; I spent 110 days on the couch/ air mattress in 4 countries last year.
